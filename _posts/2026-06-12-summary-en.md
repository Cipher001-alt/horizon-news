llama.cpp release b9606 introduces support for EAGLE3 speculative decoding, a technique that uses a lightweight draft model to predict multiple tokens ahead, which are then verified by the target model in a single forward pass. This release also includes various fixes and adaptations for Gemma4 and other architectures. EAGLE3 can achieve 2-6x speedup in LLM inference, significantly reducing latency for compatible models. This makes llama.cpp more competitive for real-time applications and broadens the adoption of speculative decoding in the open-source community. The EAGLE3 draft model uses Llama-style transformer layers and is trained to minimize KL divergence against the target model's logits. This release also includes fixes for multi-sequence issues, layer input extraction, and encoder handling, as well as support for Gemma4 EAGLE3 from RedHatAI.

github · github-actions[bot] · Jun 12, 08:45

**Background**: Speculative decoding is an inference optimization technique that accelerates large language models by predicting and verifying multiple tokens simultaneously, reducing latency while preserving output quality. llama.cpp is a popular open-source C++ implementation for running LLMs locally on various hardware, and it already supports several speculative decoding strategies. EAGLE3 is a state-of-the-art algorithm that uses a lightweight draft model to autoregressively predict multiple tokens ahead, which are then verified by the target model in a single forward pass.

It represents a significant advance in precision oncology by leveraging CRISPR's programmable targeting to kill cancer cells while sparing healthy ones. The National Academies of Science is preparing an expert report on climate attribution, which could determine liability for fossil fuel companies in lawsuits. This has sparked concerns about political interference in the traditionally nonpartisan institution. This report could influence climate litigation and policy, potentially holding fossil fuel companies accountable for extreme weather events. The outcome may set a precedent for how scientific advice is politicized in the U.S. The report focuses on attribution science, which quantifies the role of human-caused climate change in extreme weather. Fossil fuel companies are worried the findings will support liability claims in ongoing lawsuits.

## [AI Data Centers Have Minimal Total Water Use but Local Impact](https://arstechnica.com/ai/2026/06/when-it-comes-to-total-water-use-ai-data-centers-are-a-drop-in-the-bucket/) ⭐️ 6.0/10

A recent analysis reveals that AI data centers account for a negligible fraction of total global water consumption, but their local water usage can be significant. This nuanced perspective counters alarmist narratives about AI's water footprint, helping policymakers and the public understand that while aggregate impact is small, local water stress remains a concern. Even moderately sized data centers can have an outsized local impact on water resources, particularly in water-stressed regions.

**Background**: Data centers require large amounts of water for cooling servers, which has raised environmental concerns. However, total water use by AI data centers is often compared to other sectors like agriculture or thermoelectric power, where it appears small.
